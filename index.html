Lemonade Stand

Promoting the spirit of giving and entrepreneurship in neighborhood youth.

11-13 year old kids do not have a safe and reliable outlet to earn money and or give back to their community and neighbors. Create a way for parents and neighbors to connect and help support youth within their neighborhood.

Brief

Lemonade Stand is a safe and effective way for kids to earn money and awards through jobs and participate in community service projects in their neighborhood. Parents and neighbors work together to provide kids with these opportunities in order to learn valuable life lessons including giving, financial responsibility, and entrepreneurship.

Problem

Life lessons in your backyard! Learn, Earn, Save, Give

11-13 year old kids do not have a safe and reliable outlet to earn money and or give back to their community and neighbors. Create a way for parents and neighbors to connect and help support youth within their neighborhood.

User Research

User Interviews

We conducted user interviews to get a better understanding of neighbors desires to help kids earn money and how. For the parents, we wanted to understand desire to connect in their community, teach their child about money, and any potential pain-points or hesitations surrounding this concept.

Online Survey

Completed an online survey using social media consisting of eight required questions and an additional eight only for parents.

Key take-aways

  • The willingness to help neighborhood kids is high
  • Safety is a big concern for parents, lack of trust with neighbors they haven't met
  • Important for neighbors and parents to meet in order to build trust
  • Only positive reviews for kids but offer option for constructive feedback/li>
  • Only offer jobs outside and not in the home
Persona

Lemonade Stand has three target users; parents, their kids, and neighbors. Due to the limited timeframe we decided to focus on the parent persona for this study. Amanda Dunn is based off user research and is used as reference for the remainder of the study.

  • Meet other neighbors
  • Have Jacob well-liked by her neighbors
  • See Jacob learn more independence and responsibility
  • Inspire Jacob to want to do more outside the home

  • Teach Jacob how to budget, save and learn the value of money
  • Be sure Jacob is safe when out of the house
  • Other ways for Jacob to earn money other than his allowance
  • To know how Jacob is doing/behaving in and out of school

  • Trusting strangers
  • Jacob is sedentary and plays a lot of video games
  • Challenging meeting neighbors
  • Hard to incentivize Jacob

Conclusion

Based on real feedback we know that this solution could be helpful for parents and their kids, especially in today’s world. It was a rewarding and eye-opening study to hear parents perspectives and feedback from community members on the pros and cons of a potential app like this for kids.
